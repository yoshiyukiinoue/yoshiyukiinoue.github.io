Black holes, MeV gamma rays, neutrinos, and the lunar radiation environment: our group works on high-energy astrophysics through both theory and multi-messenger observations (see [Research](../research/)). Whatever position you are interested in, feel free to reach out by email. Lab visits and online chats are always welcome.

## Postdocs {#postdoc}

Our lab can host [JSPS Postdoctoral Fellows](https://www.jsps.go.jp/j-pd/), and we welcome discussions of research proposals. Researchers from abroad can apply through the [JSPS International Fellowship programs](https://www.jsps.go.jp/english/e-fellow/index.html); our group has hosted international JSPS fellows before. We are also happy to discuss hosting under other fellowship schemes.

## Graduate students (MSc / PhD) {#grad}

We accept graduate students through the [Graduate School of Engineering and Science](https://www.shibaura-it.ac.jp/examinee/graduate/index.html) at Shibaura Institute of Technology. Applicants from other universities are welcome.

- Admissions: [application guidelines](https://www.shibaura-it.ac.jp/examinee/graduate/guideline.html) (in Japanese)
- Financial support: schemes such as the [JST SPRING program](https://www.shibaura-it.ac.jp/extra/spring/index.html) are available, and research-assistant (RA) positions funded by the lab's [JST FOREST grant](https://www.jst.go.jp/souhatsu/) are also possible; please contact us for details
- Research topics range from theory and simulations to observational data analysis, and are chosen together based on your interests

## Undergraduate students {#undergrad}

Our lab belongs to the [College of Systems Engineering and Science](https://www.shibaura-it.ac.jp/faculty/systems/index.html). Undergraduate research assignments are announced through guidance sessions, and cross-departmental research assignments are also possible, so students from other departments and courses are welcome to get in touch. If you are curious about space and physics, feel free to talk to us even before the assignment period.
